# F5 Electric Soaring — Generally Applicable Rules

**Mid-level parent** for **F5J, F5K, F5L**. Inherits everything in
[00-general-rules.md](00-general-rules.md); this file records only what is common to
the F5 electric thermal classes and distinct from F3. Source: *Volume F5 Electric*
(2026 ed.2).

> Note: the volume's §5.5.1/5.5.2 "General/Contest Rules" are scoped primarily to
> F5B (multi-task) — see the note at §5.5.2. F5J/F5K/F5L each restate their own
> general rules, so the commonalities below are distilled from those three classes,
> not lifted from §5.5.1.

---

## 1. Pilot assignment to groups (the draw)

As [parent §1](00-general-rules.md#1-pilot-assignment-to-groups-the-draw). All three
F5 classes are man-on-man, rounds subdivided into frequency-compatible groups,
composition varied per round, followed by a fly-off of the top qualifiers. Group-size
minima and fly-off cuts are per-class.

---

## 2. Data the timer / helper collects

Common to F5, distinct from F3:

- **Electric-motor launch + AMRT.** Every model carries an EDIC-approved
  **Altimeter / Motor-Run Timer (AMRT)** that (a) limits/monitors the motor run and
  (b) records the **launch/start height**. The motor is used **only for launching**
  and must not run before the start signal.
- **AMRT is initialised on the ground** at the launch spot before the working time
  (sets the zero reference); the timekeeper observes the initialisation.
- **Launch/start height is recorded per flight, to the whole metre.** How it affects
  the score differs by class:
  - **F5J** — height is a *deduction* (higher launch = larger penalty).
  - **F5K** — height gives a *bonus or penalty* relative to a Nominal Launch Height.
  - **F5L** — height is a *hard cap only* (90 m, enforced by the logger); it does
    **not** enter the score.
- **Flight time precision: whole seconds** across all three F5 classes (F5J truncates;
  F5K/F5L count full seconds, tenths not rounded). This is the key contrast with
  F3J/F3K (0.1 s).
- Up to **three models** may be used. **One helper** is permitted (F5J `5.5.11.2 b`,
  F5K `5.5.10.8`); **F5L allows two** (`5.5.12.4 c`).

---

## 3. Group score

Man-on-man normalisation to 1000 as in
[parent §3](00-general-rules.md#3-group-score-normalisation), applied to a raw score
that combines flight-time points, landing bonus (where used) and the class-specific
launch-height term. Rounding of the normalised score is per-class.

---

## 4. Round & final score

- Round score = normalised group score.
- **Drop-worst thresholds differ:** F5J drops the lowest round beyond 4 rounds; F5L
  beyond 5; F5K from 7 rounds. See per-class docs.
- Fly-off and team classification as in the parent.

---

## 5. Re-flights

The [parent §7 pattern](00-general-rules.md#7-re-flights-common-pattern)
applies; per-class docs are authoritative on entitlements:

- **F5J** grants re-flights for launch/flight collisions, un-judged attempts
  and unexpected events; its new-group placement minimum is **6** re-flyers
  (the other classes use 4).
- **F5K** explicitly grants **no re-flight for a mid-air collision**;
  entitlement is organiser's fault, an un-judged attempt, or — the F5-specific
  ground — the **AMRT failing to record the launch altitude** (or the AMRT
  software being unable to determine it).
- **F5L** (provisional rules) states entitlement only — collision, or an
  event beyond the competitor's control — with **no placement priorities or
  better-of scoring rule written**; see [f5l.md](f5l.md).
- **Un-judged attempts** (F5J/F5K): the competitor is entitled only if the
  helper/pilot **informed the timekeeper of the model's position** a
  reasonable time before landing.
