# F3 Soaring — Generally Applicable Rules

**Mid-level parent** for **F3B, F3J, F3K**. Inherits everything in
[00-general-rules.md](00-general-rules.md); this file records only what is common to
the F3 soaring classes and distinct from F5. Source: *Volume F3 Soaring* (2025 v2).

> In the F3 volume each class is self-contained (`F3B.x`, `F3J.x`, `F3K.x`); the
> cross-class rules below are the CIAM General Rules plus the patterns shared by the
> three classes.

---

## 1. Pilot assignment to groups (the draw)

Common to F3B/F3J/F3K, in addition to [parent §1](00-general-rules.md#1-pilot-assignment-to-groups-the-draw):

- **Frequency-based grouping is explicitly permitted** for F3B/F3J/F3K — "frequency
  will follow frequency" is allowed, unlike other CIAM classes (`C.16.2.6`).
- **Non-spread-spectrum** pilots must nominate **multiple frequencies** (count is
  per-class); the organiser assigns one for the contest and may use any nominated
  frequency when building the flight matrix.
- Same-team pilots should not be drawn into the same group **nor the immediately
  following group** where possible (`C.16.2.6`).

Group-size minima, matrix rules and fly-off cuts are per-class.

---

## 2. Data the timer / helper collects

Common to F3, distinct from F5:

- **No launch-height data.** F3 gliders have no motor and no altimeter/motor-run
  timer; launch is by **electric winch / hand-tow line** (F3B, F3J) or **hand launch**
  (F3K). Nothing analogous to the F5 AMRT start-height is captured.
- **Flight time** and, where the task uses it, **landing distance** are the core
  fields. Precision is **not** uniform across F3 — see per-class docs (F3J and F3K
  record to **0.1 s**; F3B records duration in whole seconds and speed to 1/100 s).

---

## 3. Group score

Same normalisation principle as [parent §3](00-general-rules.md#3-group-score-normalisation).
F3J and F3K produce one normalised group score; **F3B normalises per task** (see
[f3b.md](f3b.md)).

---

## 4. Round & final score

- Round score as in the parent (F3B = sum of three partial scores).
- Drop-worst thresholds differ: **F3B** discards the lowest *partial* per task beyond
  5 rounds; **F3K** drops the lowest round from 6 rounds; **F3J** drops the lowest
  round beyond **7** qualification rounds (`F3J.3.1 a`). See per-class docs.
- Fly-off and team classification as in the parent.

---

## 5. Re-flights

The [parent §7 pattern](00-general-rules.md#7-re-flights-common-pattern)
applies, but the three F3 classes diverge sharply on **what earns a
re-flight** — the per-class docs are authoritative:

- **F3B and F3J** (tow-launched) grant re-flights for launch/flight
  **collisions and towline interference**, un-judged attempts, and unexpected
  events outside the competitor's control.
- **F3K** grants a re-flight **only for an organiser's fault** — an in-flight
  mid-air collision earns **no re-flight and no penalty**, *except* when one
  model is still in the **start phase** (release → highest point). A
  spectator moving/retrieving a model also entitles a new working time.
- **Rain interruption:** on resumption the group that was flying receives a
  re-flight (F3B Tasks A/B, F3K; F3B Task C resumes per-pilot).

Placement priorities and the better-of / official-score rule follow the
parent pattern (new-group minimum **4** re-flyers in both F3J and F3K).
